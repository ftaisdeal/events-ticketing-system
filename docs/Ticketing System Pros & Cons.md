# **Ticketing System Pros & Cons**

Here’s the trade-off: the **value of avoiding third-party ticketing fees** versus the **value of audience trust and friction-free purchasing**.

Below is a practical analysis from both sides, plus some hybrid strategies that many successful small–mid-size productions use.

## **The Importance of a Familiar Ticketing System to Sales**

### **1. Trust and Perceived Legitimacy Matter a** ***Lot\***

For most audiences, especially for an unfamiliar or new production, ticket-purchase behavior is influenced heavily by *risk perception*. Well-known platforms (Eventbrite, Ticketmaster, Brown Paper Tickets, etc.) give buyers the reassurance that:

- Payment processing is secure
- Refund/cancellation policies are clear
- Tickets will be delivered correctly
- Customer support exists

Even if your production is high-quality, an **unfamiliar checkout domain** can trigger the “Is this legit?” reflex for a certain percentage of buyers.

**Summary:** Reputable ticketing platforms automatically borrow their trust to you.

### **2. Friction in Checkout Directly Reduces Conversion Rates**

Industry-wide data is extremely consistent: *every additional step or moment of hesitation reduces sales*.
 Common drop-off points with custom systems:

- Confusing seat selection
- Slow load time
- Unfamiliar payment page
- Not recognizing the URL
- Payment failures due to weak gateways
- No Apple Pay / Google Pay
- Limited mobile optimization

Large ticketing providers have spent millions optimizing checkout flows.
 A homemade system—even a good one—rarely matches that conversion rate.

**Summary:** Even small friction (extra clicks, slow pages, non-polished UI) can drop sales by 5–25%.

### **3. Buyer Psychology Favors Familiar Logos at Payment Time**

People are far more willing to complete a purchase when they see:

- **Stripe Checkout**
- **PayPal**
- **Shopify**
- **Eventbrite/Eventbee**
- **Ticketmaster**
- **Apple Pay**

If you build your own system but rely on a trusted third-party *payment gateway*, you regain most of the psychological trust.

**Summary:** You can build a custom ticketing experience while still using a recognizable payment brand.

## **The Case for Building Your Own (Pros & Cons)**

✔ **Pros**

- Zero or minimal per-ticket fees
- Full control over branding
- Ability to integrate with your CRM, mailing lists, marketing funnels
- Customizable seat maps, pricing logic, bundles
- Long-term asset for repeated productions

✘ **Cons**

- Lower initial trust with buyers
- Slower checkout (unless you invest heavily in UI/UX)
- Customer service burden is on *you*
- Hard to match uptime, load-balancing, and anti-fraud protection
- Higher risk of bot purchases and fraudulent cards
- Must manage PCI compliance (if you don’t use Stripe Checkout)

**Summary:** Building your own system is a long-term investment, not the simplest path to maximize ticket sales *right now*.

## **How Much Does a Non-Familiar System Actually Hurt Sales?**

For a first-time production with no established brand, expect:

- **2–10% fewer completed purchases** due to perceived risk alone
- **5–20% fewer completed purchases** when checkout friction is added
- **Higher customer support load (“Is this real?” “I didn’t get my ticket”)**

For an established brand with a loyal audience, the penalty is far smaller:

- **0–3% drop-off** if the system looks polished and uses Stripe/PayPal
- Many audiences never notice who the ticketing provider is

The more *unknown* your production, the more audiences lean on familiar infrastructure for confidence.

## **Recommendation Tailored to a New Major Production**

My professional recommendation for maximizing both revenue and trust:

✔ **Hybrid Approach (Best of Both Worlds)**

1. **Build your own ticketing UI**
   - Fully branded
   - Smooth UX
   - Seat maps if needed
   - Analytics baked in

1. **Use Stripe Checkout or PayPal for final payment**

- Removes PCI compliance issues
- Gives audience instant trust
- Recognizable and mobile-optimized
- Lower fees than ticketing platforms

1. **Use a reputable PDF ticketing library for delivery**

- Clean, reliable delivery is crucial

1. **Optionally partner with one “official platform” for overflow**

- For example: sell 90% through your site and list a limited Eventbrite inventory
- This increases legitimacy and discoverability while keeping most fees off your books

This is a common model for independent theaters, operas, and immersive productions.

## **Bottom Line**

**Using a known ticketing provider can increase conversion for new audiences**, especially for a production that hasn’t yet built a reputation.

However, you **do not need to use a full third-party ticketing service** if you:

- Use a trusted payment processor (Stripe/PayPal)
- Create a polished, mobile-friendly checkout
- Communicate legitimacy clearly (SSL, branding, reviews, social proof)

**The trust penalty of a custom system can be small—if the system looks professional.**
